# Game Design Document — Missile Command Math

## Overview
Missile Command Math is a retro arcade educational game for grades 3–5 (ages 8–11) set during the 1983 Cold War era. Incoming missiles each display a math problem; the player operates a single bottom-center launcher loaded with a visible **answer queue**. The player taps a missile whose problem matches the currently loaded answer to fire and destroy it. All problems are procedurally generated by `shared/math-engine/` and the wave is pre-solved — the queue always contains exactly the answers needed, in shuffled order. Math skills escalate across 10 levels from single-digit addition (Level 1) through mixed operations and square roots (Level 10), aligned to CCSS-M grades 3–5.

## Game Loop
1. **Interstitial card** displays: historical narrative headline + mechanic briefing for any new threat/skill. Player taps "TAP TO LAUNCH →" to proceed.
2. **Wave starts**: missiles, bombers, paratroopers, and/or MIRVs spawn according to level config.
3. **Player scans** the answer queue strip (left = next to fire) and incoming missile problems.
4. **Player taps a missile** whose answer matches the currently loaded queue round.
5. **Launcher fires**: defensive missile streaks to target → explosion → positive feedback (score pop, city celebration if city was threatened).
6. **Queue advances**: next answer slides into the launcher; the cycle repeats.
7. **Wrong tap** (loaded answer ≠ missile's answer): launcher flashes red, low buzz, no queue advance.
8. **Wave ends** when all threats are destroyed or all 6 cities are lost.
9. **Level Complete / Game Over** screen shown; stars awarded; cities rebuild; proceed to next interstitial.

## Win / Lose Conditions

| Condition | Trigger | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Level Win | All threats destroyed, ≥ 1 city standing | Star rating shown; advance to next level |
| 3-Star | All 6 cities survive + bomber intercepted (if present) | Full city firework display |
| 2-Star | 4–5 cities survive | Partial celebration |
| 1-Star | 1–3 cities survive | Minimal celebration; cities rebuild |
| Level Lose | All 6 cities destroyed | Game Over screen; retry or level select |
| Session Victory | All 10 levels completed | INF Treaty victory narrative + final score summary |

Stars are influenced by: cities surviving (primary), chain reactions achieved, bomber interceptions, and accuracy percentage.

## Scoring Model

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Standard missile destroyed | +10 |
| Missile destroyed (city-threatening) | +10 + city-save bonus |
| City saved (trajectory line to surviving city) | +25 |
| Bomber intercepted (before payload drop) | +100 |
| Bomber destroyed (after payload drop) | +40 |
| MIRV destroyed before split | +60 |
| MIRV child destroyed | +15 |
| Paratrooper neutralised by blast radius | +15 |
| Chain reaction (per additional link beyond 1st) | +20 per link |

**Combo / multiplier rules:** A consecutive-intercept streak multiplies base intercept points: ×1 (1–2), ×1.5 (3–4), ×2 (5–9), ×3 (10+). Streak resets on any miss or wrong-tap. Chain reaction badges (`CHAIN x2`, `CHAIN x3`, …) stack additively with streak multipliers.

## Level Progression

| Level | Skill Type | Difficulty | Time Limit (s) | Enemy Count | Special Rule |
|-------|-----------|------------|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | Add, Subtract | Intro | 120 | 10 | Training wave precedes; 2 simultaneous max; 0.5× speed |
| 2 | Add, Subtract | Easy | 120 | 12 | Queue scan briefing; 3 simultaneous; 0.6× speed |
| 3 | Add, Sub, ×2/×5/×10 | Easy | 130 | 14 | New math intro; 3 simultaneous; 0.7× speed |
| 4 | Add, Sub, Multiply (full) | Medium | 140 | 16 | Full ×12 table; 4 simultaneous; 0.8× speed |
| 5 | All above + Division | Medium | 150 | 18 | Division intro; 4 simultaneous (held); 0.9× speed |
| 6 | All above + Fractions | Medium | 160 | 20 | Fractions intro; Bomber appears; 4 simultaneous (held); 1.0× speed |
| 7 | All above + Multi-digit | Hard | 170 | 22 | Paratrooper intro; 5 simultaneous; 1.1× speed |
| 8 | All above + √ | Hard | 180 | 24 | MIRV intro; Square roots; 5 simultaneous; 1.2× speed |
| 9 | All types | Hard | 190 | 26 | All threats active; 6 simultaneous; 1.3× speed |
| 10 | All types, mixed ops | Expert | 210 | 28 | Final wave; all threats; 6 simultaneous; 1.5× speed |

*Time limit is a soft cap — wave ends when all enemies are cleared or all cities fall. Timer loss not implemented; time limit governs max spawn window.*

## Difficulty Scaling

`DifficultyManager` applies a global difficulty multiplier (Easy = 0.7×, Normal = 1.0×, Hard = 1.3×) on top of per-level base values. Adjustable parameters and their ranges:

| Parameter | Min | Max | Controlled By |
|-----------|-----|-----|---------------|
| Missile descent speed | 0.35× | 1.95× | `baseSpeedMultiplier` × level factor × difficulty multiplier |
| Max simultaneous threats | 2 | 6 | Per-level config; unaffected by difficulty selector |
| Queue highlight assist | on | off | Player toggle (default on); difficulty selector does not force off |
| Explosion chain radius | 40 px | 80 px | `chainReactionRadius` config; not scaled by difficulty |
| MIRV split altitude | 30% | 50% | `splitAltitudePercent`; higher levels push split earlier |
| Bomber payload count | 2 | 3 | Level config; not scaled by difficulty |

Math type pool is determined by level only — difficulty selector affects kinetic pressure, not cognitive scope, keeping curriculum alignment predictable for teachers.

## Accessibility Requirements

- Minimum touch target size of **60 × 60 px** for launcher, queue items, and missile tap zones (`WCAG 2.2 SC 2.5.8` target size).
- All feedback has a **visual equivalent** — no information conveyed by sound alone (`WCAG 2.2 SC 1.1.1` / `1.3.3`).
- **Trajectory lines** on by default so missile-to-city threat is always visually explicit — no reliance on timing intuition alone.
- **Queue highlight assist** on by default; subtly colour-matches loaded answer to the corresponding missile problem.
- Math problem text rendered at ≥ **24 px**, high-contrast (white on dark or dark on light), meeting `WCAG 2.2 SC 1.4.3` AA contrast ratio (4.5:1 minimum).
- **CRT scanline effect off by default**; toggled in settings to avoid contrast degradation and motion sensitivity issues (`WCAG 2.2 SC 1.4.4`).
- **Pause button** always visible in HUD; pausing incurs no game penalty (`WCAG 2.2 SC 2.1.1` keyboard equivalent deferred to v2).
- **Sound toggle** persists via `localStorage` (user preference); default on but game is fully playable muted.
- **Portrait and landscape** orientations both supported; layout reflows via config, not fixed pixel positions.
- Interstitial cards never auto-dismiss — explicit "TAP TO LAUNCH →" button required, preventing unintended game start.

## Open Questions

- **Queue strip length:** Default of 6 visible rounds is assumed. Too few limits planning; too many crowds small screens. Needs playtesting validation before locking the `queueVisibleAheadCount` config value.
- **Paratrooper future evolution:** Brief leaves open whether paratroopers should carry math problems (making them directly shootable) at higher levels, or remain blast-radius-only throughout. Current GDD models them as blast-radius-only; any change would require a new entity variant and scoring entry.
- **DEFCON DOGGO mascot:** Brief notes unanimous design-team support for a dog mascot living in the launcher base with reactive animations. Excluded from this GDD pending an Art Direction decision and asset specification — this is a scope addition, not a clarification.
- **Bomber queue highlight on appearance:** Brief asks whether the queue round matching the bomber's bonus problem should be visually highlighted when the bomber enters. Current GDD assumes highlight assist covers this via the existing colour-match system, but an explicit bomber-specific highlight may be warranted. Needs design decision.
- **City rebuild animation timing:** Brief specifies a "rebuilding crane" animation between levels but does not resolve whether it plays out in real time (5–10 s, skippable) or snaps back instantly. GDD defers to `rebuildCitiesBetweenLevels: true` config; duration and skip behaviour need a Creative Director decision before `LevelCompleteScene` is built.
