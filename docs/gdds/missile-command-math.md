## Overview
Missile Command Math is a retro educational arcade game for grades 3â€“5 (ages 8â€“11) framed inside a
warm CRT television bezel in a mid-century illustration style. Three launchers sit at the bottom of
the playfield, each pre-loaded with an answer number. Bombs descend from the top carrying math
problems; the player taps a bomb to fire the launcher whose answer matches, producing a starburst
explosion. Twenty levels escalate from single-digit addition through mixed multi-operation challenges,
introducing each new CCSS-M skill deliberately before increasing kinetic pressure. Strategic bombers

and coding standards.

3. **Bombs descend** â€” 1â€“6 simultaneous bombs fall toward building clusters with dotted trajectory trails; a bomber may cross at high altitude (Level 13+).
4. **Player targets** â€” player taps a bomb; the launcher whose answer matches fires automatically.
5. **Hit** â€” starburst explosion fires, solved equation flashes in burst, score pop appears, launcher reloads from remaining answer pool.

## Accessibility Requirements

- Minimum 60 px touch targets on all bombs and launcher tap zones (WCAG 2.2 AA â€” 2.5.5)
- Math problem text on bombs: minimum 24 px, high-contrast against bomb body color (â‰¥ 4.5:1 ratio â€” WCAG 2.2 AA 1.4.3)
- Launcher answer numbers: minimum 32 px, white on colored launcher body (â‰¥ 4.5:1 â€” WCAG 2.2 AA 1.4.3)

- Trajectory trails always shown by default; player always knows which city each bomb threatens
- Pause button permanently visible; pause incurs no time or score penalty

- Portrait and landscape layouts both supported; HUD remains fully legible in both orientations
- Launchers and bombs are never color-matched â€” all matching is math-based, ensuring no colorblind disadvantage (WCAG 2.2 AA 1.4.1)
